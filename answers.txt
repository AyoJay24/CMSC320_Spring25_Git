Q3: What is the difference between Merging and Rebasing?

Answer: Merging keeps the history of both branches that are being merged. In other words, the history of both branches are tied together and a new commit is added to your history. Meanwhile, Rebasing overwrites a branch's history, avoiding unnecessary merge commits and therefore a cleaner history.
